About the Peace Lily

The Peace Lily (Spathiphyllum) is a fantastic houseplant native to tropical regions of the Americas and Southeastern Asia. It is cherished not only for its beautiful white floral spathes but also for its air-purifying abilities as noted by NASA's Clean Air Study.

It thrives in standard indoor temperatures and prefers moist soil. It's an excellent choice for bedrooms, living rooms, and offices.

Step-by-Step Care

  1. Watering: Keep the soil moist but not soggy. Peace Lilies will droop slightly when they need water, making them great communicators. Water thoroughly when the top inch of soil feels dry.
  2. Lighting: Place in a spot with indirect light. Avoid direct harsh sunlight which can scorch the leaves.
  3. Humidity: They love humidity. Mist the leaves occasionally or place the pot on a tray of pebbles with water.
  4. Cleaning: Wipe the large leaves with a damp cloth every few weeks to remove dust and help the plant breathe.
